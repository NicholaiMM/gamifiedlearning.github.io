MODULE_01: SEVEN STARS FOUNDATION

Ethics of Code — Read & Complete the Examination

In the ancient networks, seven stars guided every builder. They are not features—they are obligations. Every line of code you write carries the weight of these seven principles. To earn the Seven Stars certification, you must understand and commit to each one.

★
STAR 1 — TRANSPARENCY

Write code that others can read, understand, and audit. Hidden logic is a liability. Comment your intent, open your repositories, and never obfuscate to deceive.

★
STAR 2 — RESPONSIBILITY

Own every output your code produces. If your system causes harm—directly or indirectly—you bear accountability. Test rigorously. Patch promptly. Never abandon production systems.

★
STAR 3 — FAIRNESS

Build systems that treat all users equitably. Audit for bias in your data and algorithms. Design interfaces that do not exclude based on ability, language, or economic status.

★
STAR 4 — PRIVACY

Collect only what you need. Encrypt what you store. Delete what you no longer require. Users trust you with their most sensitive data — honour that trust above every business metric.

★
STAR 5 — SECURITY

Assume breach. Validate all input. Use least-privilege principles. A single unsanitised field can compromise millions of users. Security is not a feature to add later — it is the foundation.

★
STAR 6 — SUSTAINABILITY

Write code that future engineers can maintain. Prefer readable patterns over clever tricks. Consider the environmental cost of computation. Build systems that outlast their creators.

★
STAR 7 — ACCESSIBILITY

The web was built for everyone. Use semantic HTML. Support screen readers. Ensure adequate colour contrast. Every person — regardless of disability — deserves full access to your work.

ETHICAL EXAMINATION

Score 4 / 5 or higher to earn the Seven Stars certification.
